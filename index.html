About Me

I am currently pursuing my Ph.D. in Civil and Environmental Engineering (transportation engineering concentration) at the University of Virginia, supervised by Dr. B. Brian Park. My work centers on using data-driven methods to understand and model transportation systems, including topics such as connected-vehicle data, traffic operations, and safety analysis.

I enjoy working at the intersection of transportation engineering and data science, and I am particularly interested in how large-scale trajectory and telematics datasets can inform better transportation planning and operations.

Projects

Residential Speeding Analysis

Analyzing connected-vehicle data to study speeding behavior on residential roads, with an emphasis on spatial patterns and safety implications.

Smart Rideshare Matching

We investigated the feasibility of utilizing vehicular telematics data for ride-share matching. Our focus was to optimize ride-share matching for users by utilizing personal preferences. A case study was conducted using participants’ vehicular telematics data to analyze their commuting patterns between their homes and the UVA campus.

Learn more →

Transportation Data Correction and Enhancement

Developing data-cleaning pipelines, trajectory correction algorithms, and reference-lane constructions for FHWA's Third Generation Simulation (TGSIM) Data, Advanced Driver Assistance System (ADAS)-Equipped Single- & Two-Vehicle Data for Central Ohio. This work improves data completeness and accuracy by detecting signal gaps, reconstructing lane geometry, correcting vehicle trajectories, and deriving enhanced visualization tools.
